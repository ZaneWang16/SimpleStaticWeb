Fonts

Serif Fonts

  • Georgia: A classic serif font designed for screen readability, often used in digital publications and websites.
  • Bodoni: A stylish serif font known for its high contrast between thick and thin strokes, evoking a sense of elegance and sophistication.
  • Baskerville: A transitional serif font with refined details and balanced proportions, suitable for both print and digital design.
  • Garamond: A timeless serif font with a classic appearance, characterized by its graceful curves and moderate contrast.

Sans-serif Fonts

  • Helvetica: A versatile sans-serif font known for its clean and modern appearance, widely used in branding, signage, and print design.
  • Arial: A popular sans-serif font designed for clarity and legibility, commonly used in digital interfaces and publications.
  • Roboto: A contemporary sans-serif font designed for digital environments, featuring a balanced and geometrically consistent design.
  • Open Sans: A friendly and accessible sans-serif font optimized for screen readability, suitable for various design applications.

Script Fonts

  • Scriptina: A graceful and flowing script font with elegant letterforms, often used for invitations, branding, and decorative purposes.
  • Lobster: A bold and playful script font with a retro-inspired design, adding personality and charm to designs.
  • Great Vibes: A sophisticated script font with delicate strokes and swashes, ideal for elegant and formal designs.
  • Pacifico: A casual and whimsical script font with a relaxed and friendly vibe, suitable for informal and creative projects.

Commonly used text elements

<h1> to <h6>

'h1' to 'h6' elements represent headings of different levels, with 'h1' being the highest level (most important) and 'h6' being the lowest level (least important).

Headings are used to structure the content hierarchy of a webpage, with 'h1' typically used for the main heading or title, and subsequent levels used for subheadings or sections.

Search engines use heading elements to understand the structure and importance of content on a webpage, so it's important to use them appropriately.

<p>

The 'p' element is used to define paragraphs of text.

Paragraphs are the primary way to structure and present blocks of text on a webpage, making content easier to read and understand. You can use multiple 'p' elements to separate different paragraphs of text.

<a>

The 'a' element is used to create hyperlinks, allowing users to navigate to other webpages, documents, or sections within the same page. You specify the destination of the link using the href attribute, and you can optionally provide link text between the opening and closing 'a' tags.

<span>

The 'span' element is an inline container used to apply styles or scripting to a specific section of text within a larger block of content. It's often used to target and style individual words or phrases within a paragraph or heading without affecting the entire block of text.

<strong> and <em>

The 'strong' element is used to indicate text that should be given strong importance or emphasis. By default, it's typically displayed in bold. The 'em' element is used to indicate text that should be emphasized, usually displayed in italics by default.

These elements help convey meaning and importance to certain parts of text, improving readability and accessibility.
